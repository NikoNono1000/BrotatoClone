1. Boomers (Baby Boomers)

Definition: People born roughly between 1946 and 1964.

Why the name: They were born during the "baby boom" — a large increase in births after World War II.

Common traits (generalized): Grew up during times of economic prosperity; often value hard work, stability, and loyalty.

Cultural markers: Television became widespread, civil rights movements, early rock & roll.

2. Generation X (Gen X)

Definition: People born roughly between 1965 and 1980.

Why the name: The “X” reflects uncertainty or independence — they came after the well-defined “Boomers.”

Common traits: Known for being independent, resourceful, and sometimes skeptical; grew up during major changes in technology and society.

Cultural markers: Rise of personal computers, MTV, end of the Cold War, early internet.

3. Millennials (Generation Y)

Definition: People born roughly between 1981 and 1996.

Why the name: They came of age around the turn of the millennium (year 2000).

Common traits: Often described as tech-savvy, socially conscious, and team-oriented; grew up with the internet and social media.

Cultural markers: Smartphones, 9/11, social networking, Great Recession.

4. Generation Z (Gen Z)

Definition: People born roughly between 1997 and 2012.

Why the name: They follow Millennials in the alphabet (Y → Z).

Common traits: Digital natives — grew up with smartphones and social media from a young age; often value diversity, individual expression, and mental health awareness.

Cultural markers: TikTok, climate activism, online learning, streaming culture.
